Inflation Notes

    Our universe appears highly homogeneous and isotropic on large scales. However, standard FLRW cosmology predicts that the early universe contained many causally disconnected regions that could not exchange information. Despite this, these regions exhibit nearly identical temperatures and densities. This inconsistency is known as the horizon problem. Inflation proposes a period of accelerated expansion in the early universe, which explains the observed large-scale homogeneity and isotropy.
